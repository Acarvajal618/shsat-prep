Circumference of an Inscribed Circle

Inscribed circle touches all four sides — its diameter equals the square's side length.

The Problem

What is the circumference of the circle inscribed in a square with side length 6?

E 2π
F 3π
G 6π ✓ answer
H 9π

The Strategy

Core idea

A circle inscribed in a square is tangent to all four sides. Its diameter equals the side length of the square. Use C = πd to find the circumference.

When you'll see this

  • 🔍"Inscribed in a square" → diameter = side length of the square.
  • 🔍Circumference = π × diameter (or 2πr where r = d/2).
  • 🔍Answer choices in terms of π — leave the answer in exact form.

Step-by-Step Solution

  1. 1
    Identify the diameter of the inscribed circle.
    A circle inscribed in a square touches all 4 sides. Its diameter = side length of the square.
    diameter = 6
  2. 2
    Apply the circumference formula.
    C = π × d = π × 6 = 6π
✅
C = 6π → Answer: G
Radius r = 3. C = 2πr = 2π(3) = 6π ✓

Common Mistakes

  • ❌
    Using radius = 6 instead of diameter = 6 Computing C = 2π(6) = 12π — not an answer choice. The side length equals the DIAMETER (d=6), so r=3.
  • ❌
    Picking 3π (answer F) Using C = πr = π(3) = 3π — confusing the circumference formula. C = 2πr = πd, not πr.
  • ❌
    Picking 9π (answer H) This is the area of the circle: π(3²) = 9π. Don't confuse area (πr²) with circumference (2πr).
