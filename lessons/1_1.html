Averages (Weighted Mean)

Multiply each value by its frequency, add them up, divide by the total count.

Video Walkthrough

The Problem

What is the mean price paid by the 100 customers in the table above?

Price Paid # of Customers
$2550
$4030
$10020
A $25
B $33.33
C $44.50 ✓ answer
D $55

The Strategy

Core idea

For each row in the table, multiply the price by the number of customers. Sum all those products, then divide by the total number of customers to get the weighted mean.

When you'll see this

  • 🔍A table with values and frequencies screams "weighted mean" — regular averaging ignores the frequencies.
  • 🔍"Mean price paid" = total money paid ÷ total customers, not (25+40+100)÷3.
  • 🔍Check that frequencies add up to the stated total before computing.

Step-by-Step Solution

  1. 1
    Multiply each price by its customer count.
    50 × $25 = $1,250
    30 × $40 = $1,200
    20 × $100 = $2,000
  2. 2
    Sum the products (total revenue).
    $1,250 + $1,200 + $2,000 = $4,450
  3. 3
    Divide by the total number of customers.
    $4,450 ÷ 100 = $44.50
✅
$44.50 → Answer: C
Verify frequencies: 50+30+20 = 100 ✓. Revenue: 1250+1200+2000 = 4450. Mean: 4450/100 = $44.50 ✓

Common Mistakes

  • ❌
    Averaging the prices directly Computing (25+40+100)/3 = $55 — that ignores how many customers paid each price. Always weight by frequency.
  • ❌
    Picking $33.33 (answer B) This is 100/3 — no clear path here, but it's a trap for students who misset up the problem.
  • ❌
    Forgetting to divide by 100 Stopping at $4,450 total revenue without dividing by the number of customers. The mean requires dividing.
