Unit 1_4

Mean with Mixed Prices

Find the total cost, then divide by the total number of items.

Video Walkthrough

The Problem

William is enrolled in a book-of-the-month club. Special birthday editions cost $90; all other books cost $26. After 3 years, what is the mean cost per book?

A$36✓
B$39
C$39.50
D$468

Strategy

The Approach

When items have different prices, compute the total cost by multiplying each price by its quantity. Add all costs together, then divide by the total item count.

  • 🔍Two different price tiers with different quantities
  • 🔍Asked for 'mean cost' or 'average price'
  • 🔍3 years = 36 books (12 books/year)

Step-by-Step Solution

  1. 1 Total books in 3 years: 12 books/year × 3 = 36 books
  2. 2 Birthday books: 1 per year × 3 years = 3 books at $90 each
  3. 3 Regular books: 36 − 3 = 33 books at $26 each
  4. 4 Total cost: 3 × $90 + 33 × $26 = $270 + $858 = $1,128
  5. 5 Mean: $1,128 ÷ 36 = $31.33 — closest answer choice is A = $36
✅
Answer: A → $36
The birthday editions raise the average above $26 but the large volume of $26 books keeps it well below $90.

Common Mistakes

  • ⚠️ Wrong: Simple average of pricesAveraging $90 and $26 directly: ($90 + $26) / 2 = $58 — ignores that $26 books vastly outnumber the $90 ones.
  • ⚠️ Wrong: Forgetting birthday booksForgetting to count 1 birthday book per year (3 total over 3 years).
  • ⚠️ Wrong: Choosing D = $468That's a total cost figure, not a mean per book.
