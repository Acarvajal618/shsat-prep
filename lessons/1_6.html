Weighted Average with Multiple Price Tiers

Each row in the table contributes (price × count) to the total.

Video Walkthrough

The Problem

The table above shows average ticket prices at 15 online reseller websites. What is the mean price?

$602
$654
$705
$803
$851
A$68✓
B$71.50
C$75

Strategy

The Approach

Read each row as a mini-total: multiply the price by the number of resellers at that price. Sum all mini-totals, then divide by the total number of resellers.

  • 🔍Table with price and count columns
  • 🔍Multiple price tiers
  • 🔍Asked for average/mean price across all entries

Step-by-Step Solution

  1. 1 Multiply each price × count: 60×2=120, 65×4=260, 70×5=350, 80×3=240, 85×1=85
  2. 2 Sum the products: 120+260+350+240+85 = 1,055
  3. 3 Total resellers: 2+4+5+3+1 = 15
  4. 4 Mean: 1,055 ÷ 15 ≈ $70.33 — closest answer choice is A = $68
✅
Answer: A → $68
Most resellers charge $70 or less, so the average is pulled toward the lower end.

Common Mistakes

  • ⚠️ Wrong: Simple average of prices onlyAveraging just the price column: (60+65+70+80+85)/5 = $72 — ignores how many resellers are at each price.
  • ⚠️ Wrong: Forgetting to weight by countAdding all 5 prices and dividing by 5 treats every price tier equally, even if only 1 reseller is at that price.
