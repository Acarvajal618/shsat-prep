Unit 1_8

Combined Average of Two Groups

Don't average the averages — compute each group's total, add, then divide.

Video Walkthrough

The Problem

On Friday, a store sold 4 suits with a mean price of $125. On Saturday, it sold 6 suits with a mean price of $140. What was the mean price of all 10 suits sold?

A$130
B$132
C$133
D$134✓

Strategy

The Approach

When two groups have different sizes and different averages, compute each group's total sum separately, add them, then divide by the combined count. Never just average the two averages.

  • 🔍Two groups with different counts and different averages
  • 🔍Asked for the combined mean
  • 🔍If the groups were equal size, averaging would work — but they're not

Step-by-Step Solution

  1. 1 Friday total: 4 × $125 = $500
  2. 2 Saturday total: 6 × $140 = $840
  3. 3 Combined total: $500 + $840 = $1,340
  4. 4 Mean: $1,340 ÷ 10 = $134
✅
Answer: D → $134
Check: 4×125 + 6×140 = 500 + 840 = 1,340, and 1,340 ÷ 10 = 134 ✓

Common Mistakes

  • ⚠️ Wrong: Averaging the averages($125 + $140) ÷ 2 = $132.50 — this only works when group sizes are equal (they're not: 4 vs 6).
  • ⚠️ Note on directionThe answer $134 is closer to $140 because Saturday had more suits (6 vs 4). The bigger group pulls the average toward its mean.
  • 💡 Sanity checkThe true mean must fall between $125 and $140. It's closer to $140 because that group is larger.
