Unit 2_1 · Plug & Solve

Two-Variable Substitution

One value is given. Plug it in, simplify, and solve for the other.

The Problem

If x = 2 and 2x(2y − x) = 16, what is the value of y?

A 1
B 3 ✓ answer
C 6
D 12

The Strategy

Core idea

You're given the value of one variable. Substitute it everywhere that variable appears, then simplify the expression step by step until only one unknown remains. Solve for it.

Step-by-Step Solution

  1. 1
    Write down what you know.
    You're told x = 2. The equation is 2x(2y − x) = 16.
    💡 Before doing anything, circle every x in the equation — those are all getting replaced.
  2. 2
    Substitute x = 2 everywhere.
    Replace both x's:
    2(2)(2y − 2) = 16
  3. 3
    Multiply the constants outside the parenthesis.
    2 × 2 = 4, so:
    4(2y − 2) = 16
  4. 4
    Distribute the 4.
    4 × 2y = 8y and 4 × 2 = 8
    Result: 8y − 8 = 16
  5. 5
    Isolate the variable term.
    Add 8 to both sides:
    8y = 24
  6. 6
    Solve for y.
    Divide both sides by 8:
    y = 24 ÷ 8 = 3
✅
y = 3 → Answer: B
Plug y = 3 back in: 4(6 − 2) = 4 × 4 = 16 ✓

Common Mistakes

  • ❌
    Forgetting to replace every x Writing 2(2)(2y − x) = 16 — only substituting one x. Scan the whole equation.
  • ❌
    Wrong order of operations Distributing before multiplying outside constants, leading to 4y − 4 = 8 instead of 8y − 8 = 16.
  • ❌
    Picking 12 (answer D) Solving 8y = 16 (forgetting to add 8 first). Always finish isolating before dividing.
  • ✅
    Pro move: back-substitute to verify Plug x = 2 and y = 3 into the original. If it equals 16, you're done with confidence.
