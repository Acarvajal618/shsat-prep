Substitute a Fraction for a Variable

y is a fraction. Substitute carefully and simplify the complex fraction.

The Problem

If 1/y = x/3, and y = 1/5, what is the value of x?

A 1/15
B 3/5
C 5/3
D 15 ✓ answer

The Strategy

Core idea

When the given value is a fraction, substitute it directly and simplify by multiplying both sides by the appropriate value to clear the fraction from the denominator.

When you'll see this

  • 🔍y is given as a fraction — substituting creates a complex fraction (fraction inside a fraction).
  • 🔍Simplify by multiplying numerator and denominator, or by finding the reciprocal.
  • 🔍Answer choices include the reciprocal as a trap — be deliberate about which variable you're solving for.

Step-by-Step Solution

  1. 1
    Substitute y = 1/5 into 1/y.
    1 / (1/5) = 5
    So the equation becomes: 5 = x/3
  2. 2
    Solve for x.
    Multiply both sides by 3:
    x = 15
    💡 Dividing by a fraction = multiplying by its reciprocal: 1 ÷ (1/5) = 1 × 5 = 5.
✅
x = 15 → Answer: D
1/(1/5) = 5, and 15/3 = 5 ✓

Common Mistakes

  • ❌
    Treating 1/y as 1 × y = y Substituting 1/y = 1/5 instead of computing 1 ÷ (1/5) = 5. Pay attention: it's one divided by y, not one times y.
  • ❌
    Picking 3/5 (answer B) Getting x/3 = 1/5 → x = 3/5 by just substituting y wrong. The left side is 1/y = 5, not 1/5.
