Rates with Unit Conversion

Convert units carefully, then divide distance by time.

The Problem

Lucia speed walks 3 kilometers in 2/3 of an hour. At that rate, how many meters does she speed walk per minute?

A 0.75
B 75 ✓ answer
C 750
D 7500

The Strategy

Core idea

Pay close attention to units. Convert everything to the units the answer requires (here: meters and minutes), then compute the rate by dividing.

When you'll see this

  • 🔍"Per minute" in the answer → convert hours to minutes before dividing.
  • 🔍"Meters" in the answer → convert km to meters (×1000) before dividing.
  • 🔍Answer choices that differ by factors of 10 signal a unit-conversion trap.

Step-by-Step Solution

  1. 1
    Convert kilometers to meters.
    3 km × 1,000 = 3,000 meters
  2. 2
    Convert hours to minutes.
    (2/3) hour × 60 min/hr = 40 minutes
  3. 3
    Compute the rate.
    3,000 meters ÷ 40 minutes = 75 meters per minute
✅
75 meters per minute → Answer: B
Check: 75 m/min × 40 min = 3,000 m = 3 km ✓

Common Mistakes

  • ❌
    Forgetting to convert km → meters Computing 3 ÷ 40 = 0.075. The answer choices include 0.75 as a trap for this error (answer A).
  • ❌
    Using hours instead of minutes Computing 3000 ÷ (2/3) = 4500 — not matching any answer. Convert to minutes first.
  • ❌
    Picking 750 (answer C) Forgetting to convert km to m but converting hours to minutes: 3 ÷ 40 × 10000? Stay systematic with unit labels.
