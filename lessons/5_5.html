Unit 5_5

Multiples: Inclusion-Exclusion

Multiples of A but not B = multiples of A − multiples of lcm(A,B).

Strategy

Core Approach
To count multiples of 10 that are NOT multiples of 15, use inclusion-exclusion: count multiples of 10, subtract the ones that are ALSO multiples of 15. The latter are multiples of lcm(10,15)=30.

Step-by-Step Solution

  • 1
    Count multiples of 10 up to 300: 300 ÷ 10 = 30.
  • 2
    Find lcm(10,15): 10=2×5, 15=3×5. lcm = 2×3×5 = 30.
  • 3
    Count multiples of 30 up to 300: 300 ÷ 30 = 10.
  • 4
    Multiples of 10 but not 15: 30 − 10 = 20.
    Shortcut: Every group of 3 consecutive multiples of 10 (e.g. 10, 20, 30), exactly 1 is also a multiple of 15. So 2 out of every 3 qualify. 30 × (2/3) = 20.
✅
Answer: A → 20
Check: 10✓, 20✓, 30✗(mult of 15), 40✓, 50✓, 60✗, 70✓, 80✓, 90✗... pattern repeats every 30.

Common Mistakes

  • ✗ Mistake Counting multiples of 15: 300/15=20 — wrong, that's multiples of 15 not the answer.
  • ✗ Mistake Using lcm=150 instead of 30.
