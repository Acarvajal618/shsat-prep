Unit 5_6

Counting Factors of a Number

The number of factors = product of (exponent+1) for each prime.

Strategy

Core Approach
To find how many integers divide evenly into 130, first find the prime factorization. Then use the factor-count formula: if n = pa × qb × rc, the number of factors = (a+1)(b+1)(c+1).

Step-by-Step Solution

  • 1
    Prime factorization: 130 = 2 × 65 = 2 × 5 × 13.
  • 2
    Each prime appears once: 130 = 2¹ × 5¹ × 13¹.
  • 3
    Number of factors: (1+1)(1+1)(1+1) = 2×2×2 = 8.
    Formula: n = p^a × q^b × ... → factor count = (a+1)(b+1)×... Always verify by listing when the number is small enough.
  • 4
    List to verify: 1, 2, 5, 10, 13, 26, 65, 130. Count = 8 ✓
✅
Answer: A → 8 factors
130/x is an integer exactly when x is a factor of 130.

Common Mistakes

  • ✗ Mistake Thinking 130 is prime or has fewer factors because it's not small.
  • ✗ Mistake Forgetting to include 1 and 130 themselves.
