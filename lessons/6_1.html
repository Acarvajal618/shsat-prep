Unit 6_1 — Percentage: Finding a Difference

The Problem

In a pond of 1,600 fish, 25% of the fish are trout. The remaining fish are bass. How many fewer trout are there than bass?

A 200
B 400
C 800 ✓ answer
D 1200

The Strategy

Core idea

Read the question carefully. Here the question asks for the DIFFERENCE between two groups, not the size of one. Compute both, then subtract.

When you'll see this

  • 🔍"How many fewer X than Y?" → you need the difference, not just X.
  • 🔍Percentage given for one group → subtract from 100% to get the other.
  • 🔍A common trap: the answer equals the count of trout (400) rather than the difference (800).

Step-by-Step Solution

  1. 1
    Calculate the number of trout.
    1,600 × 0.25 = 400 trout
  2. 2
    Calculate the number of bass.
    1,600 − 400 = 1,200 bass
  3. 3
    Find the difference.
    1,200 − 400 = 800
✅
800 fewer trout → Answer: C
Check: 400 + 1200 = 1600 total ✓. Difference: 1200 − 400 = 800 ✓

Common Mistakes

  • ❌
    Stopping at the trout count Getting 400 (answer B) — this is the number of trout, but the question asks for the DIFFERENCE between bass and trout.
  • ❌
    Picking 1200 (answer D) This is the number of bass — again, not the difference. Re-read "how many fewer trout than bass."
  • ❌
    Subtracting wrong direction Computing 400 − 1200 = −800 and writing 800 is fine in magnitude, but conceptually: bass − trout = 800 fewer trout.
