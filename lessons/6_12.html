Unit 6_12

Repeated Percent Decrease — City Population

Find the percent decrease rate, then apply it to the next period.

Strategy

The Approach

Find the percent decrease from 2016→2018, then apply that same rate to the 2018 population to predict 2020.

  • 🔍Percent decrease = (old − new) / old.
  • 🔍Use 2016→2018 as specified; don't use 2014→2016.
  • 🔍Apply the same rate to 2018 population.

The Problem

Based on the graph above, if the percent decrease from the 2018 population to the 2020 population of City X is the same as the percent decrease from the 2016 population to the 2018 population, what should the 2020 population in City X be?

YearPopulation

Step-by-Step Solution

  1. 1
    Percent decrease 2016→2018.
    (500,000 − 400,000) / 500,000 = 100,000 / 500,000 = 20%
  2. 2
    Apply 20% decrease to 2018 population.
    400,000 × 0.80 = 320,000
✅
Answer C — 320,000
20% of 400,000 = 80,000. 400,000 − 80,000 = 320,000 ✓

Common Mistakes

  • ❌
    Simple subtractionSubtracting 100,000 again gives 300,000 — that's not a percent calculation.
  • ❌
    Wrong periodUsing 2014→2016 instead of 2016→2018 as specified.
  • ✅
    Right moveAlways use the correct base (500,000) for the percent calculation, then apply that rate to the new base (400,000).
