Unit 6_13

Tolerance — Greatest Allowable Thickness

Maximum = target + (error% × target). Don't forget to add back the original.

Strategy

The Approach

The greatest allowable thickness = target measurement + (error percentage × target). Calculate the error amount first, then add it to the original.

  • 🔍Error amount = 2% × 0.4 = 0.008 cm.
  • 🔍Maximum = original + error = 0.4 + 0.008.
  • 🔍Don't stop at just the error — add it to the target.

The Problem

A brass washer used in a mechanical device is intended to have a thickness of 0.4 centimeter, with an allowable error of 2%. What is the greatest allowable thickness of the brass washer?

A0.008 cm
B0.048 cm
C0.408 cm✓
D0.48 cm

Step-by-Step Solution

  1. 1
    Calculate the error amount.
    2% × 0.4 = 0.008 cm
  2. 2
    Maximum thickness = original + error.
    0.4 + 0.008 = 0.408 cm
✅
Answer C — 0.408 cm
0.008 is 2% of 0.4. 0.4 + 0.008 = 0.408 ✓

Common Mistakes

  • ❌
    Stopping at the errorA = 0.008 cm — that's just the error amount, not the final thickness.
  • ❌
    Using 20% instead of 2%D = 0.48 cm — that's 0.4 + 0.08, a 20% error, not 2%.
  • ✅
    Right moveMaximum = original + error. Always add back the original value.
