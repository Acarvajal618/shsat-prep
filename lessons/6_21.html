Unit 6_21

Compound Percent Decrease — Evaporating Water

Each day's 10% is taken from a smaller amount — total loss is less than 30%.

Strategy

The Approach

Each day, multiply the remaining amount by 0.9 (keeping 90%). After 3 days, the factor is 0.9³. This is compound decay, not simple subtraction.

  • 🔍Day 1: ×0.9 → 90%.
  • 🔍Day 2: ×0.9 → 81%.
  • 🔍Day 3: ×0.9 → 72.9% ≈ 73%.

The Problem

The amount of water in a bucket evaporates in such a way that the amount left at the end of each day is 10% less than the amount at the beginning of that same day. Approximately what percent of the original amount of water is left after three days?

A30%
B67%
C70%
D73%✓

Step-by-Step Solution

  1. 1
    Day 1: 10% evaporates.
    100% × 0.9 = 90%
  2. 2
    Day 2: another 10% of what remains.
    90% × 0.9 = 81%
  3. 3
    Day 3: another 10% of what remains.
    81% × 0.9 = 72.9% ≈ 73%
✅
Answer D — 73%
0.9³ = 0.729 ≈ 73% ✓

Common Mistakes

  • ❌
    Simple subtractionC = 70%: subtracting 10% three times (30% total) — ignores the compound effect.
  • ❌
    Inverted thinkingA = 30%: confusing the amount lost (≈27%) with the amount remaining.
  • ✅
    Right moveCompound decay: each day's 10% is taken from a smaller base, so total loss is less than 30%.
