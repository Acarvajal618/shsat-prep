Unit 6_22

Watermelon Rows — Floor the Percentage

Find Farmer A's %, apply to Farmer B's rows, then floor (round down) the result.

Strategy

The Approach

Find Farmer A's percentage. Apply that same percentage to Farmer B's 16 rows. Since the answer must be a whole number of rows "without exceeding," always floor (round down).

  • 🔍Farmer A: 14/20 = 70%.
  • 🔍Farmer B at 70%: 70% × 16 = 11.2.
  • 🔍"Without exceeding" → floor to 11 (not 12).

The Problem

Two farmers each have a watermelon patch of equal size. Farmer A's patch is divided into 20 equal rows, and Farmer B's patch is divided into 16 equal rows. If Farmer A harvests 14 rows of his watermelon patch, what is the greatest number of whole rows that Farmer B can harvest from his patch without harvesting a greater percentage of a patch than Farmer A harvested?

A9
B10
C11✓
D12

Step-by-Step Solution

  1. 1
    Farmer A's harvest percentage.
    14 / 20 = 70%
  2. 2
    Apply 70% to Farmer B's 16 rows.
    70% × 16 = 11.2
  3. 3
    Floor to the largest whole number that doesn't exceed 70%.
    floor(11.2) = 11 rows
✅
Answer C — 11 rows
11/16 = 68.75% ≤ 70% ✓. 12/16 = 75% > 70% ✗

Common Mistakes

  • ❌
    Rounding upRounding 11.2 up to 12 — that gives 12/16 = 75%, which exceeds 70%.
  • ❌
    Direct comparisonUsing 14/16 directly without converting to percentages first.
  • ✅
    Right moveAlways floor (round down) when "without exceeding" is the condition.
