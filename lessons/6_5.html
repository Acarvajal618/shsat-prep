Unit 6_5

Percent Decrease

% decrease = (original − new) ÷ original × 100

Strategy

The Approach

Percent change is always relative to the ORIGINAL value. Divide the amount of change by the original (before) value, not the new value. Then multiply by 100.

  • 🔍Two values: before and after
  • 🔍Asked "by what percent did it decrease"
  • 🔍Always divide by the ORIGINAL (starting) value

The Problem

Electricity usage in March was 5,000,000W. In April it was 4,000,000W. By what percent did usage decrease?

E20%✓
F25%
G50%
H75%

Step-by-Step Solution

  1. 1
    Find the change.
    5,000,000 − 4,000,000 = 1,000,000W
  2. 2
    Divide by the ORIGINAL.
    1,000,000 ÷ 5,000,000 = 1/5 = 20%
✅
E → 20% decrease
1/5 = 20%. New value = 80% of original. ✓

Common Mistakes

  • ❌
    Wrong denominatorDividing by the NEW value: 1M ÷ 4M = 25% — that measures going from April back to March.
  • ❌
    Direction confusionConfusing increase/decrease direction.
  • ✅
    The Rule% change = change ÷ ORIGINAL × 100. Always use the starting value as the denominator.
