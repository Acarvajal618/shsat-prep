Unit 6_8

Tax on Food Only

Separate taxable and non-taxable items before applying the percentage.

Strategy

The Approach

Tax applies to food only, not drinks. Separate food from drink, apply 6% to the food subtotal, then add everything together.

  • 🔍Identify which items are taxable (food) vs. non-taxable (drinks).
  • 🔍Calculate tax on the food subtotal only.
  • 🔍Add food + tax + drink for the final total.

The Problem

Patricia ate lunch at a restaurant yesterday. She ordered three items: a salad, a bowl of soup, and a glass of iced tea. The salad cost $3, the soup cost $4, and the iced tea cost $1. If there is a 6% tax on food but not on drinks, how much did Patricia pay for lunch?

A$7.00
B$8.42✓
C$8.48
D$9.00

Step-by-Step Solution

  1. 1
    Food subtotal.
    Salad + soup: $3 + $4 = $7
  2. 2
    Tax on food only.
    $7 × 0.06 = $0.42
  3. 3
    Drink (no tax).
    Iced tea = $1.00
  4. 4
    Grand total.
    $7 + $0.42 + $1.00 = $8.42
✅
Answer B — $8.42
$7 food + $0.42 tax + $1 drink = $8.42 ✓

Common Mistakes

  • ❌
    Taxing the drink too$8 × 0.06 = $0.48 → $8.48 — that's choice C, a deliberate trap.
  • ❌
    Forgetting tax entirely$3 + $4 + $1 = $8 — ignores the 6% altogether.
  • ✅
    Right moveRead carefully: tax applies to FOOD items only. Separate first, then tax.
