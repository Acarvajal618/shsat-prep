Unit 7_2

Quarter Circle Area minus Triangle

Shaded = quarter circle − right triangle.

Strategy

The Approach

The arc connects two vertices of the square, creating a quarter circle. The shaded region between the arc and the triangle equals: quarter circle area − triangle area.

  • 🔍Arc from one vertex of a square
  • 🔍Point A is center and vertex
  • 🔍Shaded region = composite: subtract the triangle

The Problem

Square ABCD has area = 100 sq m. Point A is both a vertex and the circle center. Arc BD is drawn. What is the area of the shaded region?

A100π
B(100π − 50)
C25π
D(25π − 50)✓

Step-by-Step Solution

  1. 1
    Find the side length (= radius).
    Area = side² = 100 → side = √100 = 10 m. Since A is the center, r = 10.
  2. 2
    Quarter circle area.
    Aquarter = π r² / 4 = π × 10² / 4 = 25π.
  3. 3
    Right triangle area.
    Triangle ABD has legs AB = AD = 10. A = ½ × 10 × 10 = 50.
  4. 4
    Shaded region.
    Shaded = quarter circle − triangle = 25π − 50.
✅
Answer: D → (25π − 50) sq m
Quarter circle 25π minus triangle 50 = 25π − 50.

Common Mistakes

  • ❌
    Full circle errorUsing full circle area (100π) instead of a quarter (25π).
  • ❌
    Missing subtractionForgetting to subtract the triangle area.
  • ✅
    Correct chainside = 10 → r = 10 → quarter_circle = 25π → triangle = 50 → shaded = 25π − 50.
