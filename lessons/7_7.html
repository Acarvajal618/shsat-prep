Unit 7_7

Inscribed Circle: From Circumference to Corner Area

C → r → side → area_square − area_circle ÷ 4.

Strategy

The Approach

Chain the relationships: circumference gives radius, radius gives diameter which equals square side, compute both areas, subtract for total shaded, divide by 4 for one corner.

  • 🔍Circle inscribed in square
  • 🔍Circumference given
  • 🔍Shaded = one corner of square (outside circle)

The Problem

Circle O is inscribed in square ABCD. The circumference of circle O is 4π. What is the area of the shaded region (one corner)?

Aπ − 2
B4 − π✓
C4 + π
D16 − 4π

Step-by-Step Solution

  1. 1
    Find radius from circumference.
    2πr = 4π → r = 2 → diameter d = 4.
  2. 2
    Find square area.
    Square side = d = 4. Area_square = 4² = 16.
  3. 3
    Find circle area.
    Area_circle = πr² = π × 4 = 4π.
  4. 4
    Total corners area.
    Total shaded = 16 − 4π (all four corners combined).
  5. 5
    One corner.
    One corner = (16 − 4π) / 4 = 4 − π.
✅
Answer: B → 4 − π
One corner = (16−4π)/4 = 4−π ≈ 0.86 > 0 ✓

Common Mistakes

  • ❌
    All corners mistakeD = 16−4π: that's ALL four corners, not one.
  • ❌
    Diameter vs radiusUsing diameter instead of radius in area formula: π×(4)² = 16π (wrong).
  • ✅
    Full chainC = 4π → r = 2 → side = 4 → total_shaded = 16−4π → one_corner = 4−π.
