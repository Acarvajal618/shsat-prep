Unit 8_3

Remainder Gives Position on a Cycle

Divide by cycle length. Remainder = steps into final cycle.

Strategy

The Approach

Find the cycle length (perimeter). Divide total distance by cycle length. The remainder tells you how many steps into the current (incomplete) cycle you are.

  • 🔍Travel along polygon perimeter repeatedly
  • 🔍Large total distance → final position
  • 🔍Use remainder only — quotient doesn't matter

The Problem

Pentagon ABCDE has sides of 1 cm each. A particle starts at A and travels clockwise 723 cm. Where does the particle stop?

APoint A
BPoint B
CPoint C
DPoint D✓

Step-by-Step Solution

  1. 1
    Find the cycle length.
    Perimeter = 5 × 1 cm = 5 cm per full cycle.
  2. 2
    Divide total by cycle.
    723 ÷ 5 = 144 remainder 3.
  3. 3
    Count steps from A.
    Remainder 3 → 3 steps clockwise from A: A → B → C → D.
  4. 4
    Final position.
    The particle stops at D.
✅
Answer: D → Point D
723 = 144×5 + 3. Remainder 3 → 3 steps from A → D ✓

Common Mistakes

  • ❌
    Direct positionUsing 723 directly as a position without dividing by cycle length.
  • ❌
    Off-by-one3 steps from A lands ON D (B = 1st, C = 2nd, D = 3rd).
  • ✅
    Remainder mapR=0→A, R=1→B, R=2→C, R=3→D, R=4→E.
