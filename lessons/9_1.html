Unit 9_1 — Setup & Solve an Equation

The Problem

Isabel has 10 boards that are x meters long, 5 boards that are 2x meters long, and 4 boards of lengths 3 m, 6 m, 9 m, and 13 m. All boards laid end-to-end total 271 meters. What is x?

A 5 m
B 6 m
C 10 m
D 12 m ✓ answer

The Strategy

Core idea

Separate what you know (fixed lengths) from what you're solving for (expressions with x). Subtract the known total from the grand total, then set up and solve a simple equation.

When you'll see this

  • 🔍"Laid end-to-end" = add all lengths and set equal to total.
  • 🔍Some boards have fixed lengths, others depend on x — group them separately.
  • 🔍"What is x?" means simplify to a single-variable equation and solve.

Step-by-Step Solution

  1. 1
    Sum the known (fixed) board lengths.
    3 + 6 + 9 + 13 = 31 meters
  2. 2
    Find how much is left for the x boards.
    271 − 31 = 240 meters
  3. 3
    Write the equation for the x boards.
    10x + 5(2x) = 240
    10x + 10x = 240
    20x = 240
  4. 4
    Solve for x.
    x = 240 ÷ 20 = 12 meters
✅
x = 12 meters → Answer: D
Check: 10(12) + 5(24) + 31 = 120 + 120 + 31 = 271 ✓

Common Mistakes

  • ❌
    Forgetting to distribute 5(2x) Writing 10x + 2x = 12x instead of 10x + 10x = 20x. Distribute the 5: 5 boards × 2x each = 10x.
  • ❌
    Including fixed boards in the x equation Writing 10x + 10x + 31 = 271 then solving 20x = 240 is correct — but some students include 31 in the x group and get the wrong setup.
  • ❌
    Not subtracting fixed boards first Setting 20x = 271 → x ≈ 13.55. Subtract fixed lengths (31) first: 20x = 240.
