Unit 9_4

Dumpster Cost Break-Even

Match two pricing formulas, then solve for the unknown pounds.

Strategy

The Approach

Write one cost equation for each company. Set them equal because the totals match, then solve for the pounds that make the two prices the same.

  • 🔍Two cost structures are described for the same service.
  • 🔍Both people paid the same total.
  • 🔍Look for "flat rate" versus "base fee + per-unit" wording.

The Problem

Ester and Noel each rented a dumpster. Ester's cost was $365 plus $1.25 per pound over 3,000 pounds. Noel's cost was a flat rate of $430 for up to 3,000 pounds. If both paid the same total amount, how many total pounds did Ester's dumpster contain?

A52✓
B65
C82
D292

Step-by-Step Solution

  1. 1
    Let the unknown be the overage.
    Let p be the number of pounds over 3,000 that Ester hauled.
  • 2
    Write Ester's cost formula.
    365 + 1.25p represents her flat fee plus the per-pound charge.
  • 3
    Write Noel's cost.
    Noel pays the flat $430 as long as he stays at or below 3,000 pounds.
  • 4
    Set the two costs equal.
    365 + 1.25p = 430 because they spent the same amount.
  • 5
    Solve for p.
    Subtract 365 from both sides: 1.25p = 65. Then divide: p = 65 ÷ 1.25 = 52.
  • 6
    Interpret the result.
    Ester hauled 3,000 + 52 = 3,052 pounds total, so the answer choice 52 (the overage) matches.
    ✅
    Answer: A → 52 pounds over 3,000
    365 + 1.25×52 = 365 + 65 = 430 ✓ matches Noel's cost.

    Common Mistakes

    • ❌
      Dividing 430 by 1.25 immediatelyignores the $365 base fee and produces 344 pounds, which is meaningless.
    • ❌
      Answering with 3,052the question asks for the amount OVER 3,000, not the total weight.
    • ✅
      Balance the equation firstAlways move the flat fee to the other side before dividing by the per-unit cost.
