Unit 9_8

Budget Shortfall

Find the total cost, subtract what you have, and the difference is the shortfall.

Strategy

The Approach

Compute the total price of all items separately. Compare that total to the money on hand. The shortfall is total − available.

  • 🔍Multiple items at different prices.
  • 🔍The prompt asks how much more money is needed.
  • 🔍Always subtract the amount already saved.

The Problem

Tariq has $10 and wants to buy 21 oranges at $0.30 each and 12 apples at $0.50 each. If there is no sales tax, how much more money does Tariq need?

A$2.00
B$2.30✓
C$2.60
D$12.00

Step-by-Step Solution

  1. 1
    Find the cost of oranges.
    21 × $0.30 = $6.30.
  • 2
    Find the cost of apples.
    12 × $0.50 = $6.00.
  • 3
    Add the two totals.
    Total cost = $6.30 + $6.00 = $12.30.
  • 4
    Subtract the $10 Tariq already has.
    $12.30 − $10 = $2.30.
    ✅
    Answer: B → $2.30 more
    Total cost $12.30 minus $10 on hand leaves a $2.30 shortfall.

    Common Mistakes

    • ❌
      Reporting $12.30That is the total cost, not the extra money needed.
    • ❌
      Forgetting the applesStudents sometimes multiply 21×0.30 and stop there.
    • ✅
      Label your stepsWrite "total cost" and "money on hand" so subtraction is in the correct order.
