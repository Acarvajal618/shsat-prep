Unit 9_9

Shopping Equation

Translate the money story into an equation, then solve for the item price.

Strategy

The Approach

Let each item cost x. Write “starting money − total spent = ending balance.” A debt is a negative ending balance.

  • 🔍One person buys identical items.
  • 🔍The problem mentions a debt (negative balance).
  • 🔍Two identical items means the equation will include 2x.

The Problem

If Thomas went shopping with $15, bought two items that cost the same amount, and ended up with a debt of $5, how much did each item cost?

A$5
B$10✓
C$15
D$20

Step-by-Step Solution

  1. 1
    Define the variable.
    Let x represent the price of each item.
  • 2
    Model the spending.
    Thomas buys two identical items, so he spends 2x.
  • 3
    Write the balance equation.
    Starting money − spending = ending balance → 15 − 2x = −5 because a $5 debt is −5.
  • 4
    Solve for x.
    Subtract 15 from both sides: −2x = −20. Divide by −2: x = 10.
    ✅
    Answer: B → $10 each
    15 − 2(10) = −5, matching the $5 debt.

    Common Mistakes

    • ❌
      Treating debt as +$5The equation becomes 15 − 2x = 5, which produces the wrong answer.
    • ❌
      Using 15 ÷ 2Halving the starting money ignores the fact that Thomas overspent.
    • ✅
      Explicitly mark negative balancesWrite “ending balance = −5” so the sign is right before solving.
